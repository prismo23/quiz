Lesson 1: What is Ocean Wave Energy?

Ocean wave energy, also known as wave power, captures the energy produced by surface waves to generate electricity. It is a type of renewable energy that leverages both the kinetic and potential energy of waves. As wind moves over the ocean surface, it transfers energy to the water, creating waves that can be harnessed for power generation.

Key Characteristics of Ocean Waves

- Wave Height (Amplitude): The vertical distance between the wave crest and trough.
- Wavelength: The horizontal distance between two successive crests or troughs.
- Wave Period: The time it takes for two consecutive crests to pass a given point.
- Wave Frequency: The number of wave cycles passing a fixed point per unit time.

Lesson 2: Benefits of Ocean Wave Energy

Wave energy offers several benefits as a renewable energy source:
- Sustainability: Wave energy is a sustainable and renewable source of power.
- High Energy Density: Compared to other renewable sources, wave energy has a higher energy density.
- Reduced Emissions: It helps reduce greenhouse gas emissions, combating climate change.
- Job Creation: It creates jobs in the renewable energy sector, from installation to maintenance.

Lesson 3: Types of Wave Energy Devices

Different technologies capture and convert wave energy into electricity:
- Point Absorbers: Devices floating on the surface, capturing energy from all directions.
- Oscillating Water Columns: Chambers converting wave motion into air pressure, driving turbines.
- Overtopping Devices: Structures that collect wave water and release it through turbines.
- Wave Attenuators: Snake-like structures that convert wave motion into hydraulic pressure.
